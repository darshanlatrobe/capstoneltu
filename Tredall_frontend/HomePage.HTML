South Italian Pottery

Made at Paestum, some signed by the craftsmen, starting in the mid-4th century BC.

Ancient Greek South Italian Paestan Red Figure Lebes Gamikos

From the python workshop, both sides with a female head in profile to the left, one wearing a dotted kekryphalos, the other wearing a dotted sphendone, tongues on the shoulder, palmettes below the handles, the lid with black palmettes on the upper surface, bead-and-reel on the rim.
