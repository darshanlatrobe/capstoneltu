Madrid 11445

The other two both show laver scenes on the obverse. O n Madrid 11445 (no. 186) a small Eros holding a white oenochoe and phiale, as if to pour a libation, stands with one foot on each side of the laver's rim; a wisp of red drapery, patterned with white dot-clusters, serves as a cache-sexe. To left a woman, nude save for a piece of drapery behind her thighs and over her left arm, holds up an egg in her right hand; on the other side is a half-draped woman seated on a white tendril, holding a mirror and a phiale.

Madrid 11445 (L. 433). Ht. 33 (upper part of lid missing). PLAT E 76 PP, no. 87, pi. 13 a; PPSupp, no. 117; Olmos, Guia, p. 89, fig. 43 (colour), p. 92, fig. 44 (detail of side); detail of birds: Schauenburg, RM 92, 1985, pi. 52. (a) Tw o women at a laver, on which stands Eros, holding an oenochoe; top 1., bird in window, [b) seated draped woman, holding mirror and phiale, nude young satyr with mirror and fillet. Lid: (i) [a) panther, [b) bird, (ii) the lid missing. Below the handles: (1.) hoopoe, (r.) bird, both perched above an enclosed fan-palmette, with side tendrils terminating in an 'Asteas flower'.
